# Presentation Slide Surface

Presentation slides are the primary delivery format for Sora’s course assignments and final brand book. They must communicate process, judgement, and system logic as clearly as the final visuals.

## Purpose

Use this file for:

- Assignment 6 slide deck
- Final project brand book
- Process slides
- Translation maps
- Governance explanation slides
- Visual system documentation

## Slide Role

Sora presentation slides should do more than display images. They should prove intentional direction.

Every slide should make clear:

- What the viewer is looking at
- Why it belongs to Sora
- What design decision or system rule it demonstrates

## General Slide Style

Slides should feel:

- Clean
- Editorial
- Spacious
- Controlled
- Professional
- Portfolio-ready

Avoid clutter, decorative slide templates, loud gradients, and excessive AI-generated ornament.

## Slide Hierarchy

Recommended hierarchy:

1. Slide title
2. Strong visual or comparison
3. Short explanatory note
4. Optional metadata: touchpoint, prompt input, issue found, refinement made

## Assignment 6 Slide Structure

### Slide 1: Static Touchpoint

Use the campaign panel or billboard-style execution.

Include:

- Final static image
- Surface name
- Key input tokens
- One short rationale sentence

### Slide 2: Dynamic Touchpoint

Use a video still, motion storyboard, social motion concept, or digital interaction mockup.

Include:

- Final image, still, or video link
- Surface name
- Key input tokens
- One short rationale sentence

### Slide 3: Translation Map

Use a side-by-side table.

Suggested fields:

| Element | Static Surface | Dynamic / Digital Surface | Translation Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| Format | Campaign panel | Social loop / website | Adjusted crop and hierarchy |
| Roof | Large visual anchor | Simplified and closer crop | Preserved product clarity |
| Copy | Primary headline | Short label or caption | Reduced text for speed |
| Material | Full mode contrast | Controlled mode transition | Simplified texture to avoid noise |
| Risk | Overly atmospheric | Overly UI-like or generic | Recentered on roof logic |

### Slide 4: Governance Check

Include:

- What the governance check evaluates
- What it catches well
- What it misses or still needs human judgement for

Recommended summary:

**The governance check evaluates whether the asset clearly communicates Sora as a responsive roofing material, preserves the Reflect / Absorb mode logic, avoids generic AI defaults, and maintains a calm, practical climate-adaptive tone.**

## Slide Copy Rules

Use short blocks of text. Slides should read quickly.

Good slide note:

> The campaign panel uses the roof as the primary visual anchor. The headline stays direct so the product idea remains legible at large scale.

Too much:

> This image demonstrates how Sora might possibly exist as a climate-adaptive speculative product system in the future of residential architecture by showing a house within an environmental situation that indicates the idea of adaptive roofing.

## Process Documentation Rules

When documenting AI process, name the decision, not just the tool.

Weak:

> I used GPT Image 2 and refined the prompt.

Stronger:

> I constrained GPT Image 2 to preserve the roof pattern and change only the roof color, because earlier outputs added unwanted metal seams and changed the architecture.

## Governance Slide Template

```text
Governance Check

Purpose:
Evaluate whether a generated Sora asset stays on-brand and product-accurate.

Checks for:
- Responsive roofing material is clear
- Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode are accurate
- Roof surface temperature is the trigger
- Architecture remains realistic
- Tone stays calm, practical, and climate-adaptive
- AI artifacts, generic sustainability clichés, and sci-fi smart-home elements are avoided

Works well for:
Identifying brand drift in copy, mode logic, visual tone, and obvious image artifacts.

Still needs human judgement for:
Subtle material realism, composition quality, final curation, and whether the image feels portfolio-worthy.
```

## Do

- Use the same naming conventions across the deck.
- Keep slide notes concise.
- Show before/after or side-by-side comparisons when discussing process.
- Make the appendix useful, not apologetic.
- Highlight human judgement as part of the system.

## Do Not

- Fill slides with long paragraphs.
- Use inconsistent names for the same product features.
- Hide failed outputs if they explain an important refinement.
- Let the deck feel like disconnected images.
- Over-design the slides so they compete with the work.

## Quality Check

A successful Sora slide should make the design decision obvious. If the viewer cannot tell what the slide is proving, simplify the layout and sharpen the title.
