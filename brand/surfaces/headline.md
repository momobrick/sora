# Headline Surface

Headlines are the highest-level verbal expression of Sora. They should make the product idea clear quickly while preserving the brand’s calm, adaptive, and practical point of view.

## Purpose

Use headline guidance for:

- Campaign panels
- Presentation slide titles
- Social posts
- Website hero sections
- Product explainers
- Brand book section openers

## Headline Role

A Sora headline should do one of three things:

1. Explain the product simply.
2. Express the brand belief.
3. Frame the climate-adaptation benefit without exaggeration.

## Core Headline

**A roof that responds to temperature.**

This is the clearest campaign-level statement and should be treated as the primary headline for high-impact applications.

## Headline Voice

Sora headlines should be:

- Clear
- Calm
- Direct
- Intelligent
- Grounded
- Slightly poetic only when the meaning remains obvious

Avoid headlines that are too clever, too technical, too dramatic, or too vague.

## Headline Patterns

### Product Explanation

Use when the viewer needs to understand what Sora does.

- A roof that responds to temperature.
- Roofing that adapts as conditions change.
- A surface that shifts with the climate.
- Responsive roofing for changing conditions.

### Climate Adaptation

Use when the surface needs to connect to broader environmental context.

- Built for weather that does not stand still.
- Adaptation, built into the roofline.
- A practical response to extreme temperature swings.
- Helping homes adapt to a changing climate.

### Reflect / Absorb Mode

Use when the surface shows the two-mode system.

- Reflect heat when the roof gets hot.
- Absorb warmth when the roof gets cold.
- Light when heat builds. Dark when warmth matters.
- Two modes. One responsive roof.

### Migration-Inspired Brand Language

Use sparingly for higher-level brand moments.

- Guided by the logic of migration.
- Designed to move with the environment.
- Adaptation is a natural intelligence.
- The home learns from the horizon.

## Recommended Assignment 6 Use

For the static campaign panel, use one primary headline only:

**A roof that responds to temperature.**

For the dynamic or secondary touchpoint, use a shorter headline or mode-based phrase:

- Reflect Mode
- Absorb Mode
- Two modes. One responsive roof.

## Recommended Final Project Use

Use headlines to structure the final brand book:

- The Market Gap: **Homes were built for a climate that is changing.**
- The Brand Seed: **Buildings should adapt to their environments.**
- Product Logic: **A roof that responds to temperature.**
- Visual System: **One material. Two modes.**
- Governance: **Keep the system adaptive, accurate, and controlled.**

## Do

- Lead with the product idea.
- Keep the sentence structure simple.
- Use active, concrete language.
- Connect visual shifts to temperature-based adaptation.
- Make the benefit understandable without overexplaining.

## Do Not

- Describe Sora as “smart roofing.”
- Use generic sustainability phrases such as “green,” “eco-friendly,” or “save the planet.”
- Imply the product responds to mood, time of day, forecasts, or interior temperature.
- Overpromise energy savings, cost savings, or performance outcomes.
- Use sci-fi language such as “future roof,” “AI roof,” “living skin,” or “self-aware home.”

## Quality Check

A successful Sora headline should still make sense if seen without a caption. If the headline needs too much explanation, simplify it.
