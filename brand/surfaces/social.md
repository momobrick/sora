# Social Surface

Social surfaces translate Sora into small, fast-reading formats. They must simplify the system without making it feel generic or inaccurate.

## Purpose

Use this file for:

- Feed posts
- Story frames
- Reels or short motion loops
- Carousel posts
- Small digital ads
- Assignment 6 dynamic or secondary touchpoint

## Surface Role

Social should make one idea immediately legible:

- Sora responds to roof surface temperature.
- The roof shifts between Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode.
- The product helps homes adapt to changing climate conditions.

## Recommended Formats

| Format | Best Use |
|---|---|
| 1:1 square | Product mode comparison, single concept post |
| 4:5 vertical feed | Campaign image with headline and caption |
| 9:16 story/reel | Mode transition, roof detail, motion storyboard |
| Carousel | Step-by-step product logic explanation |

## Visual Rules

Social crops should preserve:

- The roof plane as the primary subject
- Clear Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode color differences when relevant
- Realistic residential architecture
- Clean, calm compositions
- Strong figure/ground clarity

Because social formats are small, simplify detail. Reduce secondary information before reducing product clarity.

## Motion Guidance

For a short dynamic execution, use simple motion. The motion should demonstrate the product logic, not add decorative animation.

Recommended motion concepts:

1. **Mode transition wave** across the roof surface
2. **Split lighting shift** from warm/cool or day/dusk
3. **Slow roof close-up** showing the low-relief surface pattern
4. **Before / after mode comparison** with a controlled transition

Avoid motion that makes the roof feel liquid, magical, alive, electrical, or overly technological.

## Dynamic Prompt Scaffold

Use this structure when generating or describing a social/motion touchpoint:

```text
Create a short social-format Sora brand motion concept showing a residential roof responding to roof surface temperature. Preserve realistic architecture and a calm climate-adaptive tone. The roof shifts between Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode as a controlled material transition, not a magical or liquid effect. Keep the roof texture low-relief, smooth, and subtle. Use clean composition, practical residential context, and minimal on-screen text. The motion should communicate: a roof that responds to temperature.
```

## Static Social Prompt Scaffold

```text
Create a social-format Sora campaign image for a responsive roofing material. Feature a realistic residential home with the roof plane clearly visible. Show the roof as the primary visual subject. Use Sora’s calm, practical, climate-adaptive tone. Include clear visual distinction between Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode if showing the two-mode system. Avoid futuristic smart-home imagery, generic sustainability clichés, solar panels, excessive UI graphics, and decorative AI effects.
```

## Copy Hierarchy

For social, use very little copy.

1. Headline or mode label
2. One-sentence caption
3. Optional small product detail

Example:

**A roof that responds to temperature.**  
Sora shifts between Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode based on roof surface temperature.

## Carousel Structure

Use a carousel when explaining the system.

1. **A roof that responds to temperature.**
2. **Reflect Mode:** Lightens when roof surface temperature rises.
3. **Absorb Mode:** Darkens when roof surface temperature drops.
4. **Independent zones:** Each roof area can respond separately.
5. **Designed for everyday climate adaptation.**

## Assignment 6 Translation Notes

Social is useful as the second touchpoint because it forces the brand system to scale down. The main translation issue is simplification:

- The campaign panel can show a full environmental scene.
- Social should focus on one product behavior at a time.
- Small formats need stronger cropping, fewer words, and less visual texture.
- Motion tools may simplify the material pattern, so use a static style reference or close-up swatch to preserve the Sora surface quality.

## Do

- Make the roof readable at small sizes.
- Use mode labels when helpful.
- Keep motion slow, controlled, and functional.
- Use captions to clarify product logic.
- Preserve Sora’s calm and practical tone.

## Do Not

- Overload social posts with technical copy.
- Use random climate imagery that does not show the product.
- Let motion effects imply magic, electricity, or sentience.
- Add generic app icons, smart-home dashboards, or glowing sci-fi overlays.
- Use tiny roof textures that become visual noise.

## Quality Check

At thumbnail size, the viewer should understand that the roof is the subject. If the format only reads as a generic house image, the social surface is not working.
