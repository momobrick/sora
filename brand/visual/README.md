# Sora Visual Guidance

This folder defines the visual identity layer for Sora. Use these files whenever Sora’s appearance, imagery, color, typography, composition, material texture, campaign visuals, or presentation system are created or reviewed.

The goal of this folder is to keep Sora visually consistent, believable, and distinct. Sora should feel like a practical climate-adaptive building material with a calm architectural presence — not a futuristic gadget, decorative surface treatment, generic sustainability brand, or overly technical smart-home product.

## What This Folder Is For

Use the `visual/` folder when creating or reviewing:

- brand identity references
- color applications
- typography and hierarchy
- image-generation prompts
- house and landscape visuals
- material swatch studies
- roof texture and profile studies
- campaign imagery
- website, presentation, and social layouts
- icons, diagrams, and interface-adjacent visuals
- visual consistency across assignments or brand touchpoints

## Recommended Workflow

1. Start with `identity.md` to understand the overall look and feel.
2. Use `color.md` before choosing roof colors, brand accents, backgrounds, or mode-based color treatments.
3. Use `typography.md` when creating layouts, captions, slides, web copy, or campaign surfaces.
4. Use `imagery.md` when generating or selecting photography, house images, landscapes, composites, or material studies.
5. Use `material-signature.md` when showing Sora’s roof texture, panel profiles, shingle profiles, or close-up material behavior.
6. Use `composition.md` when arranging visuals across a layout or building a multi-touchpoint system.
7. Use `iconography.md` when explaining Reflect Mode, Absorb Mode, roof zones, transitions, or homeowner controls.
8. Cross-check with `../product/product-accuracy-rules.md` before finalizing any visual that shows the roof system.

## Core Visual Principles

Sora’s visual system should feel grounded in architecture, climate, and material behavior.

Prioritize:

- calm visual clarity
- practical residential realism
- material depth and tactile detail
- warm, grounded neutrals
- restrained contrast
- clear roof-mode communication
- real homes in believable landscapes
- simple, legible layouts
- subtle innovation rather than futuristic spectacle

Avoid:

- glowing tech effects
- sci-fi surfaces
- decorative patterning that feels unrelated to roof function
- overly polished AI fantasy imagery
- generic green/sustainability clichés
- excessive gradients, icons, or dashboard-like visuals
- visual complexity that makes the product harder to understand

## Material Signature

Sora’s main visual signature is a soft sculptural wave and striated material texture. This texture should feel molded into the roof material itself, not printed on top of it.

The material signature should be:

- low relief
- architectural
- subtle and durable
- softly sculptural
- integrated into each roof profile
- visible enough to be distinctive without becoming decorative
- repeated within the logic of the roof surface, seams, shingles, panels, or tiles

The wave and striation treatment should adapt to the selected roof profile. It should not erase seams, distort roof geometry, ignore perspective, or span unnaturally across unrelated roof sections.

## Imagery Direction

Sora imagery should make the product feel real, useful, and believable in everyday residential contexts.

Use imagery that shows:

- homes in real climate regions
- roof planes clearly enough to understand the material
- realistic lighting, shadows, weather, and landscape detail
- residential scale and architectural plausibility
- roof behavior as a surface-color response, not a mechanical transformation
- calm, documentary realism rather than glossy product fantasy

When showing climate contexts, the home and landscape should feel regionally appropriate. The roof should remain the focus when product behavior is being explained, but the environment should help make the use case feel real.

## Color Direction

Sora’s color system should come from the material world: roof surfaces, metal, stone, weathered neutrals, soft blacks, pale reflective grays, warm architectural tones, and one restrained brand accent.

Use color to support:

- Reflect Mode as a lighter roof state
- Absorb Mode as a darker roof state
- calm hierarchy in layouts
- accessibility and readability
- warmth without becoming rustic or decorative
- product clarity without overexplaining

Do not rely on bright green, neon blue, or generic climate-tech palettes unless explicitly called for in a specific concept.

## Typography Direction

Sora typography should feel clear, modern, and calm. Type should help explain a new product category without making the brand feel cold, technical, or overly corporate.

Use type to create:

- clear hierarchy
- concise educational moments
- confident headlines
- readable captions and product explanations
- spacious, uncluttered layouts

Avoid novelty type, overly futuristic type, excessive tracking, low-contrast text, and layouts where typography competes with the roof imagery.

## Prompting Guidance

When prompting Sora visuals, combine visual direction with product accuracy.

Use language like:

> Create a photorealistic residential image that feels calm, architectural, and practical. Show Sora’s responsive roofing material as the exterior roof surface. Preserve the roof geometry, seams, profile, lighting, shadows, and perspective. The material should include Sora’s subtle low-relief sculptural wave and fine striated texture, integrated naturally into the selected roof profile.

For campaign images, clarify whether the visual is showing:

- a normal installed roof
- Reflect Mode
- Absorb Mode
- a mode transition
- a climate-specific residential context
- a material close-up
- a split day/dusk concept
- a diagram or explanatory visual

Avoid prompt language that implies the roof glows, animates like a screen, mechanically flips, opens, peels, rotates, or behaves like a solar panel.

## Review Checklist

Before finalizing any Sora visual, confirm:

- Does it feel architectural, residential, and believable?
- Is the roof shown as the material itself, not an overlay or decorative skin?
- Is the soft wave and striated texture subtle, sculptural, and integrated into the roof profile?
- Are roof seams, zones, pitch, edges, and perspective preserved?
- Are Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode visually distinct without feeling exaggerated?
- Does the image avoid glowing, sci-fi, screen-like, or mechanical effects?
- Is the landscape realistic and regionally appropriate?
- Is the layout clear, spacious, and easy to understand?
- Is type legible and accessible?
- Does the result align with Sora’s calm, practical, climate-adaptive positioning?
