# Color

Sora’s color system is material-derived, warm neutral, and functional.

Color should communicate climate response, material behavior, and residential warmth without feeling decorative, synthetic, or generic green-tech.

The palette should feel grounded in roofing materials, natural light, stone, clay, charcoal, dusk, and warm architectural neutrals.

## Core Color Direction

Sora color should feel:

- Warm neutral
- Grounded
- Architectural
- Material-based
- Calm
- Practical
- Climate-aware
- Sophisticated but accessible

Avoid palettes that feel:

- Cool tech
- Neon
- Overly bright
- Synthetic
- Generic sustainability green
- Luxury black-and-gold
- High-contrast sci-fi
- Sterile silver-blue

## Palette Structure

Sora’s color system has four main roles:

1. Base neutrals
2. Reflect Mode colors
3. Absorb Mode colors
4. Accent color

The palette should be restrained. Most brand surfaces should use warm neutrals, with Reflect and Absorb colors used to explain product behavior.

The accent color should be used sparingly.

## Base Neutrals

Base neutrals should create a warm architectural foundation.

Use colors inspired by:

- Warm off-white
- Stone
- Clay
- Taupe
- Warm gray
- Soft charcoal
- Smoked graphite
- Natural shadow

Suggested starting palette:

- Warm Off-White: `#F4F0E8`
- Pale Stone: `#E6E0D5`
- Warm Mineral Gray: `#D0C8BA`
- Soft Taupe: `#A79C8F`
- Deep Taupe: `#71675C`
- Warm Charcoal: `#2C2924`
- Deep Graphite: `#1F211F`

These values are starting points, not final production standards. Test and adjust as needed.

## Reflect Mode Colors

Reflect Mode should feel light, warm, mineral, and heat-reducing.

Reflect colors should suggest brightness without glare.

Use:

- Warm light gray
- Soft mineral gray
- Pale stone
- Light taupe-gray
- Subtle warm highlights

Avoid:

- Pure white
- Icy silver
- Blue-gray tech tones
- Mirror-like reflectivity
- Harsh contrast
- Synthetic brightness

Suggested Reflect range:

- Reflect Light: `#E8E2D7`
- Reflect Mid: `#D2CABE`
- Reflect Shadow: `#B9B1A5`

## Absorb Mode Colors

Absorb Mode should feel deep, warm, grounded, and heat-retaining.

Absorb colors should suggest visual depth without becoming heavy or black.

Use:

- Warm charcoal
- Dark graphite
- Smoked bronze
- Deep brown-gray
- Subtle caramel or bronze undertones

Avoid:

- Flat black
- Glossy black
- Blue-black
- Metallic shine
- Plastic texture
- Heavy industrial color

Suggested Absorb range:

- Absorb Deep: `#262521`
- Absorb Mid: `#38312B`
- Smoked Bronze: `#4A3A2E`

## Accent Color

Sora uses one restrained accent color inspired by the yellow bill of the sora bird, translated into a muted architectural ochre.

Accent color:

- Muted Ochre: `#906C2B`

Use this color sparingly for:

- Emphasis
- Small callouts
- Diagrams
- Mode indicators
- Rules
- Interface details
- Key words
- Small moments of warmth

The accent should feel natural and grounded, not bright or decorative.

Avoid:

- Bright yellow
- Safety yellow
- Metallic gold
- Luxury gold
- Neon yellow
- Overuse across large surfaces

## Accessibility

Color should be used with accessibility in mind.

Do not assume a color is accessible on its own. Contrast depends on the foreground and background pairing.

The muted ochre accent may work on pure white for some text uses, but it can fall below accessible contrast on warmer off-white or pale stone backgrounds. Test contrast before using it for small text, icons, buttons, or functional interface elements.

Use darker neutrals for primary text.

Recommended text colors:

- Primary text on light backgrounds: `#1F211F`
- Secondary text on light backgrounds: `#2C2924`
- Light text on dark backgrounds: `#F4F0E8`

Use the ochre accent more safely for:

- Large display accents
- Rules
- Small non-text graphic elements
- Highlights
- Charts or diagrams with labels supported by dark text
- Accent fields on dark backgrounds after contrast testing

## Color Behavior

Color should support product meaning.

Reflect Mode should communicate:

- Heat reflection
- Reduced heat absorption
- Daylight
- Brightness
- Practical cooling support

Absorb Mode should communicate:

- Heat absorption
- Retained warmth
- Dusk or cooler conditions
- Depth
- Practical comfort support

The two modes should feel related, not like two separate products.

## Environmental Color

Environmental imagery may include:

- Warm daylight
- Dusk pinks and mauves
- Soft sky blues
- Desert clay
- Mountain greens
- Coastal sand
- Forest shadow
- Agricultural earth tones

These colors should come from realistic environments, not from decorative brand gradients.

## Avoid

Avoid color systems that rely on:

- Green sustainability clichés
- Bright climate-tech gradients
- Neon blue
- Electric green
- Chrome silver
- Pure black and white extremes
- Candy-like sunset colors
- Overly dramatic climate contrast

## Summary

Sora’s palette should feel warm, neutral, material-derived, and climate-aware.

The brand’s most important color story is the shift between Reflect Mode and Absorb Mode, supported by warm architectural neutrals and one restrained ochre accent.
