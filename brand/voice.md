# Sora Voice

Use this file when writing, rewriting, reviewing, or refining Sora copy across brand messaging, campaign language, product descriptions, website copy, social captions, presentation text, and AI-generated content.

## Voice Summary

Sora sounds clear, grounded, warm, practical, and quietly forward-looking.

The voice should make climate-adaptive building materials feel useful and emotionally relevant without sounding abstract, salesy, alarmist, overly technical, or exclusive.

## Voice Attributes

Sora’s voice should feel:

- Clear, intelligent, and emotionally grounded
- Warm but not sentimental
- Visionary but not abstract
- Innovative but not overly technical
- Hopeful but not unrealistic
- Accessible rather than elitist
- Human-centered, practical, and climate-aware
- Confident without sounding corporate or salesy
- Calm, trustworthy, and quietly forward-looking

## Tone Keywords

Grounded, responsive, resilient, intelligent, practical, adaptive, calm, optimistic, future-aware, environmental, human-centered, quietly innovative.

## Writing Principles

### Make innovation feel useful

Sora should not present innovation as novelty. Connect new ideas to everyday benefits such as comfort, efficiency, protection, resilience, and lower energy burden.

### Stay human and accessible

Use plain, direct language. Avoid jargon unless it is necessary and clearly explained. Sora should sound like a thoughtful building materials brand, not a technical white paper or software platform.

### Be hopeful without hype

Sora can be optimistic and future-aware, but it should avoid generic launch excitement or exaggerated claims. The tone should feel steady, not promotional.

### Acknowledge climate reality without fear

Sora can speak to extreme heat, cold, UV exposure, rising energy costs, and strained infrastructure, but it should not rely on fear-based or guilt-driven climate messaging.

### Keep the product grounded

When describing Sora’s offering, focus on what it does for homes, people, and communities. Avoid making the product sound like a futuristic gadget, luxury upgrade, or experimental technology.

### Use metaphor subtly

The Sora name can suggest migration, seasonal awareness, movement, adaptation, orientation, and responsiveness. These ideas should appear lightly and purposefully, not as literal bird language.

## Sentence Style

Sora copy should generally use:

- Short to medium-length sentences
- Clear subject-verb structure
- Concrete benefits
- Calm confidence
- Everyday language
- Light emotional resonance

Avoid dense technical phrasing, empty slogan language, and overly poetic abstraction.

## Example Voice Direction

Sora should sound like this:

> Climate adaptation should feel like part of home.

> Materials that work with changing conditions.

> A roof that responds to temperature.

> Built for comfort, efficiency, and everyday resilience.

Sora should not sound like this:

> The ultimate smart green roofing revolution is here.

> Welcome to the future of luxury climate technology.

> Save the planet with the most advanced roof ever created.

## Review Standard

Before finalizing Sora copy, check that it feels warm, grounded, clear, useful, and attainable. The copy should support the idea that buildings should adapt to their environments while avoiding hype, fear, elitism, and unsupported technical claims.
