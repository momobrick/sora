# Sora Audience

Use this file when defining who Sora is for, what the audience needs, what barriers they may bring, and how the brand should speak to them.

## Primary Audience

Sora speaks to homeowners and communities living in climate-variable or environmentally exposed regions who are seeking more resilient, efficient, and environmentally adaptive building solutions.

This audience includes people affected by extreme heat, cold, high UV exposure, rising energy costs, wide seasonal shifts, strained energy infrastructure, and homes that struggle to stay comfortable in changing conditions.

## Audience Contexts

Sora should feel relevant across a range of places and housing contexts, including:

- Urban neighborhoods affected by heat exposure and energy burden
- Suburban homes facing rising cooling and heating demands
- Rural communities with climate exposure, weather variability, or limited infrastructure
- Climate-variable regions with strong seasonal or day-to-night temperature swings
- Environmentally exposed regions such as desert, mountain, sunbelt, coastal, and other high-exposure areas
- Lower-income communities where inefficient housing can create a greater comfort and cost burden

## Practical Adopters

Sora is for people who want better-performing homes without needing to buy into luxury sustainability, complicated technology, or smart-home culture.

They may be interested in climate adaptation, energy efficiency, comfort, and resilience, but they do not want a product that feels experimental, exclusive, overly technical, or ideologically loaded.

They need the brand to feel:

- Practical
- Reliable
- Attainable
- Easy to understand
- Relevant to real homes
- Grounded in everyday comfort
- Useful across income levels and regions

## Emotional Needs

Sora’s audience should feel that climate adaptation can be steady, empowering, and integrated into daily life.

They should not feel blamed, overwhelmed, intimidated, or excluded. The brand should acknowledge changing climate realities without relying on fear, guilt, or crisis language.

Sora should create a sense of calm confidence: homes can be designed to respond more effectively to the conditions around them.

## Barriers to Address

The audience may be skeptical of solutions that sound too futuristic, too political, too expensive, too fragile, or too complicated.

Sora should avoid language and imagery that reinforces those concerns. The brand should not feel like a niche climate-tech product for wealthy early adopters. It should feel like a practical building material system designed for broad real-world use.

## Accessibility and Equity

Sora should be presented as a brand with relevance across income levels.

When discussing affordability or access, the brand may reference pathways such as rebates, grants, utility incentives, public-private partnerships, or affordable housing programs. These should be framed as possible access models, not guaranteed availability unless specifically confirmed.

## How Sora Should Meet the Audience

Sora should meet people with clarity, warmth, and respect.

The brand should explain climate-adaptive building materials in simple, human language, connecting innovation to comfort, resilience, protection, energy consciousness, and lower energy burden.

Sora should make the product feel like part of a home’s natural function, not like another complicated system the homeowner has to manage.
