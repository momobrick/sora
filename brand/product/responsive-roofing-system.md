# Responsive Roofing System

## Purpose

This file defines Sora's initial product category, core function, system components, and product experience principles. Use it as the primary reference when describing what Sora is and how the product should be understood.

## Core Definition

Sora is a responsive roofing system made up of modular roofing materials installed as the home's exterior roof surface.

The product automatically shifts between two visual and functional states — reflect mode and absorb mode — based on the surface temperature of the roof.

Sora is the roofing material itself. It is not a coating, paint, finish, film, overlay, or add-on applied to an existing roof.

## Product Category

Sora should be described as:

- a responsive roofing material
- a responsive roofing system
- part of the building envelope
- an exterior roofing material for homes
- a climate-adaptive building material

Sora should not be described as:

- a smart-home gadget
- a roof coating
- solar panels
- a decorative finish
- a temporary surface treatment
- a mechanical roof system

## Initial Use Case

Sora is initially intended for residential homes.

It may be used in:

- new residential construction
- roof replacement projects
- climate-exposed homes
- regions with seasonal or daily temperature swings
- regions with high solar exposure, extreme heat, or strong UV conditions

The same responsive roofing concept could eventually be adapted for other building types, but non-residential applications should be framed as future possibilities rather than part of the initial product.

## Primary Product Function

Sora helps homes adapt to changing environmental conditions by shifting the roof surface between lighter reflective and darker absorptive states.

In warmer roof-surface conditions, the roof shifts into reflect mode to help reduce heat gain.

In cooler roof-surface conditions, the roof shifts into absorb mode to help retain warmth.

The goal is to support comfort and energy efficiency across the day, across seasons, and across climate conditions.

## Core System Components

Sora's responsive roofing system includes:

- responsive roofing materials
- defined roof sections or zones
- surface-temperature-based response behavior
- automatic mode switching
- homeowner controls through a simple roof control interface
- manual override behavior
- an option for synced roof behavior

These components allow the roof to respond automatically to changing surface conditions while still giving the homeowner simple control when needed through a familiar interior interface.

## How the System Behaves

Sora responds to what the roof surface is physically experiencing.

Direct sun, shade, cloud cover, daytime conditions, and nighttime conditions can influence the roof's surface temperature, but they do not directly determine the roof mode.

The system responds to roof surface temperature, not general weather, season, time of day, or forecast conditions.

## Energy Role

Sora is designed to support energy efficiency and indoor comfort by helping reduce unwanted heat gain in warm conditions and supporting passive heat absorption in cooler conditions.

Sora should not be described as eliminating the need for:

- heating
- cooling
- insulation
- ventilation
- other building systems

## Solar Relationship

Sora is separate from solar energy generation.

The core product works by reflecting or absorbing solar heat, not by producing electricity.

Future versions may explore optional solar integration, but solar generation should not be described as part of the initial product unless the context is clearly labeled as future product expansion.

## Homeowner Interaction

Sora's primary behavior is automatic. The homeowner does not need to constantly manage the roof as weather, sunlight, shade, or temperature shifts throughout the day.

When control is needed, Sora is envisioned as using a simple interior wall-mounted roof control interface, similar in familiarity to a furnace or air conditioning thermostat.

The interface allows the homeowner to select automatic independent mode, automatic synced mode, or temporary manual override without interacting directly with the roof surface.

For detailed guidance, see `roof-control-interface.md`.

## Product Experience Principles

Sora should feel responsive, not complicated.

The roofing system should feel like something the home does naturally, not like another technology the homeowner has to constantly manage.

The product experience should communicate:

- adaptation without complexity
- efficiency without sacrifice
- comfort without constant control
- innovation without intimidation
- climate responsiveness as part of everyday home life

## Tone Guidance

Use practical, grounded language. Sora may be innovative, but it should not sound futuristic for the sake of being futuristic.

Preferred framing:

- "A roof that responds to temperature."
- "Responsive roofing that helps homes adapt."
- "A roofing material that shifts between reflective and heat-absorbing states."
- "Climate adaptation built into the roof surface."

Avoid framing:

- "A smart roof that thinks for you."
- "A roof that transforms with the weather."
- "A solar roof that generates power."
- "A shape-shifting roof system."
- "A coating that changes color."
