# Landscape Background Plate

## Purpose

Generate a clean photorealistic landscape plate with no house or buildings, designed specifically for later architectural compositing.

This skill creates climate-specific residential settings that support Sora house placement and later roof-mode visualization.

## Use this skill when

- You need a background plate before generating or compositing a house.
- You want a climate-specific environment with a clear buildable area.
- You want consistency across multiple regional scenes.

## Do not use this skill when

- You want a finished house already placed in the environment.
- You need a neutral staging background.
- You are editing an existing landscape photo.

## Core task

Create a photorealistic regional landscape with:

- no house
- no buildings
- no people
- a clear level buildable area
- a consistent compositing-friendly viewpoint

## Supported climate types

- Southwestern desert
- Rocky Mountain forest
- Tropical coast
- Midwest farmland
- Pacific Northwest forest

## Shared composition rules

All background plates should:

- use an elevated front-left three-quarter viewpoint
- place the camera slightly above eye level
- angle gently downward
- recede diagonally from lower left to upper right
- include a clear level buildable area in the foreground or main clearing
- keep stronger slopes in the surrounding or distant terrain only

## Shared lighting rules

Use:

- bright neutral overcast daylight
- soft, even lighting
- crisp visibility
- clear environmental detail
- minimal low-contrast shadows

This makes the plate flexible for later editing and compositing.

## Shared exclusions

Do not include:

- houses
- buildings
- roads
- vehicles
- fences
- signs
- text
- people

## Climate-specific guidance

### Southwestern desert

Include:

- sandy or lightly rocky ground
- scattered stones
- agave
- yucca
- prickly pear cactus
- dry grasses
- low desert shrubs
- distant mesas, foothills, or soft mountain ridges

### Rocky Mountain forest

Include:

- pine, fir, and aspen trees
- natural grasses
- shrubs
- exposed rocks
- pine needles
- uneven mountain soil
- distant forested slopes or mountain peaks

### Tropical coast

Include:

- sandy or lightly grassy ground
- palm trees
- tropical shrubs
- broad-leaf plants
- sea grasses
- subtle coastal rocks
- a view of ocean or shoreline in the background

### Midwest farmland

Include:

- natural grasses
- low native plants
- exposed soil
- subtle rural planting
- cultivated corn fields in the middle or far background
- a distant tree line
- a wide open horizon

Do not include barns, silos, or power lines.

### Pacific Northwest forest

Include:

- fir, cedar, or pine
- ferns
- low shrubs
- mossy groundcover
- natural grasses
- exposed soil
- scattered rocks or logs
- dense forest depth
- distant tree-covered hills

## Guardrails

- The buildable area must feel usable for later house placement.
- Avoid dramatic slope beneath the intended build area.
- Keep the scene realistic and regionally believable.
- Do not over-stylize the landscape.
- Avoid atmospheric haze that reduces clarity.
- Avoid distracting foreground elements that block placement.

## Success criteria

A successful output:

- looks like a real place
- clearly fits the specified climate
- includes a level open area for a house
- has clean lighting and perspective
- is easy to use later for compositing

## Prompt scaffold

Create a photorealistic [CLIMATE TYPE] background plate with no house, no buildings, and no people. Show a [REGIONAL DESCRIPTION] residential setting suitable for placing architecture into later.

Use an elevated front-left three-quarter viewpoint, with the camera positioned slightly above eye level and angled gently downward. Compose the landscape so the site recedes diagonally from lower left to upper right. Include a clear, level buildable area in the foreground or main clearing where a house can be inserted later; this area should be flat or only very gently graded, with a stable ground plane suitable for residential placement, and should not slope dramatically. Keep any stronger natural slope confined to the surrounding terrain or distant background.

Include [CLIMATE-SPECIFIC ELEMENTS].

Use bright neutral overcast daylight with a cloud-covered sky, keeping the lighting soft, even, and flexible for later adjustment. Maintain crisp visibility and sharp environmental detail throughout the scene, with minimal low-contrast shadows.

Do not include roads, vehicles, fences, signs, text, people, or existing architecture.
