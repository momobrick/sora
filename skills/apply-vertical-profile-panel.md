# Apply Vertical Profile Panel

## Purpose
Apply Sora’s vertical roof profile panel treatment to a house image with a blank roof, while preserving the original house, roof geometry, environment, lighting, and realism.

This skill is used to convert a clean blank roof into a Sora standing-seam roof surface with the correct physical profile and material texture, without changing the house design or applying mode-based color changes.

## Use this skill when
- You have a house image with a blank or simplified roof.
- You want to apply Sora’s vertical panel roof profile.
- You want to preserve the existing roof color, lighting, and architectural geometry.
- You need a roof surface suitable for later Reflect Mode / Absorb Mode treatment.

## Do not use this skill when
- You want to generate a house from scratch.
- You want to place a house into a landscape.
- You want to apply Reflect Mode or Absorb Mode color changes.
- You want to redesign the roof shape or roof structure.
- You want to generate a different roof profile such as shingles or barrel tile.

## Core task
Replace only the blank roof surface with Sora’s vertical profile panel treatment.

The application should preserve:
- roof pitch
- roof edges
- roof geometry
- roof perspective
- roof color
- lighting
- shadows
- realism

The application should change only:
- the roof’s physical surface profile
- the roof’s panel structure
- the roof’s material texture detail

## Required outcome
The final image should show:
- the same original house
- the same original landscape or staging environment
- the same roof shape and roof color
- the same lighting and perspective
- a realistic standing-seam roof panel system applied to the blank roof

## Inputs
- A house image with a blank roof
- Optional reference image showing the desired Sora vertical panel profile and texture

## Vertical profile characteristics
Apply a realistic standing-seam panel roof system with:
- raised vertical ribs
- recessed panel pans
- clean panel alignment following the roof slope
- subtle low-relief flowing wave bands
- fine striations that follow the direction of the wave bands
- durable architectural material character

The wave texture should repeat within each panel pan rather than flow continuously across the entire roof as one oversized graphic.

## Instructions
1. Use the house image as the base image.
2. Preserve the existing house design completely.
3. Preserve the roof’s existing shape, pitch, edges, scale, and perspective.
4. Preserve the roof’s existing color, lighting, and shadows.
5. Replace only the smooth or blank roof surface with the vertical panel roof profile.
6. Apply realistic raised standing seams and recessed panel pans.
7. Add subtle sculptural wave texture within each panel pan.
8. Add fine striations that follow the flow direction of the wave texture.
9. Keep the effect architectural, material-based, and integrated into the roof plane.
10. Ensure the roof still reads as a believable real roofing system rather than a decorative overlay.

## Important constraints
- Do not apply Reflect Mode or Absorb Mode coloration.
- Do not change the roof color.
- Do not make the roof darker, warmer, shinier, glossier, or more reflective.
- Do not change the house, siding, windows, doors, porch, garage, landscaping, sky, or background.
- Do not change the roof pitch, silhouette, eaves, ridge lines, or edges.
- Do not flatten the standing seams.
- Do not lose the panel depth.
- Do not turn the texture into a printed graphic or 2D surface pattern.
- Do not create repetitive AI artifacts.
- Do not let the wave pattern span unrealistically across the entire roof as one continuous graphic.
- Do not redesign the architecture.

## Guardrails
The result should feel:
- realistic
- architectural
- durable
- regionally believable
- cleanly integrated
- materially precise

The result should not feel:
- decorative
- printed
- fake
- overly glossy
- futuristic
- overly dramatic
- like a texture pasted on top of the roof

## Success criteria
A successful output:
- preserves the original house and scene
- preserves the original roof geometry
- adds clearly readable standing-seam structure
- applies subtle sculptural banding within each panel
- keeps the roof realistic and buildable
- avoids unwanted recoloring or regeneration

## Prompt scaffold
Apply only the referenced vertical roof panel’s physical pattern and texture to the blank roof of the house. Preserve the house image’s existing roof color, lighting, shadows, perspective, pitch, edges, and architectural geometry. The wave texture should repeat within each panel pan rather than span continuously across the entire roof. Keep the material realistic, architectural, durable, and fully integrated into the roof plane.
